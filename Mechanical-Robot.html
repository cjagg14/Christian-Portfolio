Mechanical Robot Project

Project Description

In this group project, I worked with 3 other members with the goal to design a 4-bar linkage walking robot. We used different ratios of wooden lengths to find the best result that allows the robot to walk straight with a fast pace. In this project we used the software SOLIDWORKS to model the robot's parts as well as Linkage Mechanism to model the movement of the linkages.

In our first iteration we wanted to have the two front legs move the whole robot while the back two legs drag behind. After testing out this design we realized that the legs were not the right size and that it would never stay upright.

We also found that we can't have any dragging parts so we began thinking of a new design.

With our second iteration, we used a similar mechanism to the previous design but added two other legs simulating a walking motion. With the design finished we then created a CAD drawing of the parts which we used in the laser cutter allowing for accurate and precise cuts.

Once everything was cut out we then assembled all the pieces to finish the model (as seen in the hero image at the top).

After the design was finished we brought it to the competition. Once there, we tested out the completed mechanism. The robot was continually catching on itself, likely due to the difference in length in the drive shaft arm. When it was our time to compete our robot didn't end up moving.

Although our design in the end failed, we learned a lot about teamwork and determination to not give up. With the first design a complete fail, we recovered ourselves quickly to produce a model that could meet the requirements to give ourselves a chance in the competition.
