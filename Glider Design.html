Glider Design Project

Project Description

In this group project I worked alongside Samson Argentino and Thanh Le with the goal to design a light but sturdy glider. In order to meet these design requirements our team collaborated ideas together until we discovered the ideal solution, going through numerous iterations in the process.

Starting off with our first complete iteration, we developed a robust design; however, it turned out to be quite heavy. This was due to the fact that we used a 3D printed tail. Although it looked sleek, the weight of the tail shifted the center of gravity closer to the rear. This caused an unnecessary pitching moment resulting in a bad glider design. We shared different ideas once again and came up with the solution to make the tail out of wood and move the wing/fuselage closer to the center.

In SOLIDWORKS I modeled the new improved design for the team. This new iteration will be lighter as well as have a center of gravity closer to the wing. This will result in a smother flight allowing the glider to reach further lengths.

Modeling in SOLIDWORKS then rendered in blender I finally reached the last iteration of the glider to show the team. This new and improved design was very light weighing around 86 grams while still giving us the compact sturdy design we desired. Taking this final iteration to the competition it placed us 6th overall out of 30 different groups.
