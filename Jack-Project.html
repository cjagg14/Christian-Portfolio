Aircraft Jack Project

Project Description

I am working alongside Samson Argentino and Gabriel Petta with the goal to design an aircraft jack for small airplanes. I used software such as SOLIDWORKS, ANSYS, and Blender to achieve the best design possible for the team.

In SOLIDWORKS I modeled the main frame of the jack which allowed me to figure out the shape and right dimensions necessary. This model can then be used in ANSYS to figure out the maximum stresses to ensure our model can withstand the load.

In ANSYS I applied a load of 15,000 lbs at the top to simulate the weight of a small airplane. We then can use these stresses to help us pick a durable, lightweight material to finalize our design.

After all the modeling is complete I then rendered the final design in Blender to simulate how it will function in the real world. In this project, we have a jack which can hold a plane up to 10,000 lbs. The jack has a factor of safety of 1.5 and it has many different functions as the base can be interchanged with different attachments (as shown in the hero image above).
